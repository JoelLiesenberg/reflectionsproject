Choco Bro's Big Dumb Game

Why yes, you hapless saps, that morbol minion you could just do like four runs of Aurum Vale for is definitely worth 500k gil. For sure, for sure. Not like a sprout’s gonna have 500k to burn anyway, but there’s a ton of rich idiots who can’t be bothered to run that poisonous hell-hole of a dungeon, so they’ll pay out the wazoo if it means they don’t need to do something.

It’s lazy assholes like that that keep those of us in business, you know. I could never commit to actually learning crafting or gathering, cause nothing sounds more boring than sitting and executing a macro thousands of times in a row. They can’t either apparently cause they’re the ones that have crafting bots up and running that do all that shit for them. And because they have no morals, they’re the ones that get rich off it, and have all that extra cash to spend on my slightly inflated goods.

Okay, okay, looks like everything’s up, now time to buy up some of the cheaper stuff to drive up the average price, and there we go, gotta spend money to make money, you know…and ha, neat, someone already took the morbol.

Let’s have a look-see around the plaza, hmm, not too many potential marks tonight, most are clearly already in parties, most are BIS, and the rest are AFK…one of the downsides of making your grifting base of operations in the endgame city, a lot less gullibility. Maybe I should log and fantasia over to my Lala girl, she’s usually pretty successful at attracting some passersby who’ll trade some gil just to see her dance and look cute, and yeah it’s a little icky, but meh, it’s all transactional.

Oh, wait, no, look, she may work…little Hyur girl, looking lost, kind of quiet, hmm, yes…probably lucky I’m already on my Hyur dude, he tends to come off as the most trustworthy, strangely enough, given his...somewhat amusing looks. Ahem.

“Excuse me, can I help you with anything?”

She’s startled, good, catching them off-guard, puts them on the back-foot immediately.

“Oh, um, hi, yes, uh…’Choco Bro…’do you happen to know what a fantasia is?”

Oh shit, I forgot I was in my chocobo suit still, fuck, should prob–wait did she just ask about fantasia? Oh fuck yeah, score. This’ll be good.

“Yes, yes, call me Choco, hmm, fantasia you say…well you came to the right place, uh…”

“Asuna.”

“Asuna, yes you’ve come to the right place. I happen to know someone who specializes in procuring that exact rarity!”

“Wait, really!? You’re serious!?”

“Absolutely! I, unfortunately, don’t know his secrets, says he’s from Meracydia or something, super hush-hush on the specifics, but I try not to ask too many questions.”

“Oh wow, um, is there a way that your friend would be able to get one for me?”

Cha-ching.

“Oh, but of course, I’m sure, though, as I assume you’ll be expecting, it unfortunately isn’t the cheapest thing.”

“That’s fine, I’ll pay anything.”

Whoa, that was fast. Usually takes a little more encouragement and schmoozing, but hey if she’s bound and determined, all the better for me. Might even be able to crack a million with her.

“Oh, that’s great, yes, yes, so the base rate I can usually work out with him is roughly 600k gil, but I don’t know when next he’ll be in the area. I know this is a big ask, but if you’d be willing and able to charter a ship, I could sail to Meracydia and find him myself?”

“How much would that cost?”

“Probably another million or so.”

“So 1.6 million gil? I’ll do it.”

What!? This chick must be loaded. Fuck it, I can totally keep pushing this.

“Well that’s great news! Great news, indeed! Though I just remembered the last time he brought me one, he said the vein where he mines the crystal dust dried up. But he did say there was another vein further down, and if he had the right tools and manpower, he could tap into it quite easily.”

“Hmm, I mean, I guess that…can’t be helped…”

“No, no, unfortunately quite necessary, I’m afraid.”

“I’ll pay it. Again, whatever it takes.”

“How…how much do you have anyway?”

“15,650,574 gil.”

Insert dramatic spit-take, I mean, holy shit, I–how far can I push this? She seems suspiciously gung-ho, gotta say…uh okay, think, think, um, I’m a lady, she’s probably a lady, cause who else would play a Hyur girl, uh–

“Will 10 million cover it?”

“Can you make it 11?”

“Deal.”

Holy fuck, she already has it in the trade window. Accept it. Oh my god. She legit just tripled my gil.

“Thank you, Asuna, I’ll be sure to give this to my friend to put to good use.”

“No, thank you, Mr. Choco, this…this is more important than you know. I’ll be awaiting news in Limsa.”

Limsa? Ew, fine, I’ll give it to her in sprout central, ugh, how annoying. Also, “Mr. Choco”? First of all, Choco’s wearing a helmet and has the last name of Bro, but still, how dare she assume. Blegh. Granted, I guess I’m assuming that she’s a she, but who cares, it’s a game. It’s a transaction.

Ope and look she’s already gone. Well then, guess I better go get a fantasia then. Oh wow, this is gonna be oh so hard.

First, teleport to the moon. Second, head to the Watcher’s tower. Third, grab a fantasia.

Oh look, I’m already done.

Now what.

I wonder what ever happened to that one dude who told me about this place? He was just some random dude out in the desert or something. I think I was grinding a FATE or an S rank hunt or, I don’t even know what I would’ve been doing out there, especially since it’s such a low level area. Anyway, here comes this dude, who’s all serious and shit and talking about how the sand named him and I was just like “okay, weirdo, can you fight?” and he just kept looking at the blasted sand instead of helping me fight. So annoying.

But then he told me something about there being a massive secret stash of stuff on the moon in the Watcher’s tower. And that’s when my ears perked up. There was actually something to this taking-game-way-too-seriously weirdo. I hadn’t done much exploration of the moon yet cause MSQ is boring as fuck, but I thought why the hell not and ported out of there.

Lo and behold, the dude was on to something, cause I search the Watcher’s tower top to bottom, and find the motherlode of fantasias, just sitting there. Guess the devs must have forgotten to patch it or something. Usually you can only get fantasia on the mogstation, so there’s really not much use in selling them in-game, but there were countless of em down there, so I changed my entire business model.

It’s these saps’ own fault if they don’t know how common and easy to get these things are. If they’re gonna get deep and analytical about some pixels, then who am I to stop them? Just give me some money first, and we can make something work!

This girl, Asuna, she’s probably only just heard of fantasias, and thinks they’re some rare, one-time only thing. And yeah, there’s a sidequest hidden away in backwater SoC or some place that gives you a free one, and it’s the only one in the game like that, but you can literally buy as many as you want with real money. Like, come on people, use your brains.

It’s what I’ve been doing this whole time. Certain people respond more to different races and genders, so I keep a little stockpile to myself, and switch Choco up as need be. I’ve got twenty saved appearances, so if I need, say, an elegant Elezen, I just log out, load up that appearance, and I’m set.

Easy peasy.

The fun part is that everyone just assumes I’m a dude, cause marketboard manipulators and trolls tend to be dudes, and I just let em think that. Hence the Bro last name. Better they hurl that kind of abuse than the abuse they’d throw if they knew I was a girl. But I’ve got thick skin. So joke’s on them. I just post their diatribes to me and my friends’ Discord server and we just laugh.

See, my friends and I, we like to think we’re the fun kind of trolls, I guess. You know, we all have stupid names, we do stupid glams, use fun mods, take the piss out of everything. Cause at the end of the day, we’re all just playing a big dumb game and having fun. No reason to take things too too seriously.

And I’m not cheating people out of real money, or anything, just in-game shit. Gil is literally worthless if you turn the power off.

*yawn*

Ugh, how much time is enough time!? Maybe I’ll log off and like make a snack or sleep or something. Also have to head out for some errands. Hope Asuna’s not stressing too much, or anything. She knows she can turn the game off and take a breather, yeah? She seemed really desperate for this.

Okay, I think that’s more than enough time. Let’s message this girl.

Cool, she says to meet her in the Drowning Wench. Guess we’re really role-playing this shady deal. May as well play it up. I think I have a glam with a dark cloak in here somewhere. Meh, sure, this’ll work.

Alright, let’s teleport over to Limsa. God, why’d they have to put the tavern so far from the aetheryte? Limsa got it right with the marketboards being right there, but the fact that the tavern, and therefore the inn, the only place where you can access your glamour dresser, mind you, is stupidly far, is a travesty of epic proportions and I won't stand for it, except I will cause while I may be a troll, I am a powerless troll.

Ugh, okay, there she is. Sitting over in the back. *clears throat dramatically* Ahem.

“You have it?”

Alright, right down to business, she really wants this thing. Jeez. Guess I’ll slink into the seat across from her and act all kooky and mysterious or whatever. All the game’s a stage, all the Hyurs and Cat-things merely players.

“Aye, aye I have it. Had to bust a few heads over at the docks cause my friend wanted to go rogue, but it’s here.”

I’ll just give her a flash of it in my bag to tantalize her. This is too fun, don’t wanna get to dessert before the main course. I just hafta find out what’s got her so worked up. Or she could just immediately reach for it. It’s like a kid with a cookie…

“Now now, no need for haste, dearie. It’s yours, paid in full, but let’s just enjoy the night a bit longer, eh?”

Her face just went…completely blank? Is…is she okay? Guess I’ll mime eating and drinking this digital gruel and just…I dunno…wait til she’s back?

Hello? Earth to Asuna? Must be some recollec–oh fuck, now she’s staring daggers at me all of a sudden. This lady’s scary. I need to get out of here. May as well come out with it.

“So why, pray tell, does a lovely little Hyur girl want something like this, hmm?”

And there she goes, instead of answering me, she just goes and looks aimlessly at anything but me. Got it. Guess I went a little too creepy with the glam? Should I have fantasiaed? Nah, that would’ve made the aire of rarity a little less precious. Gotta keep my eyes on the pr–

“Please. I’m tired. I’ve already paid. Just give it to me.”

Holy fuck, this girl’s close to tears. Jeez this is…this is getting way too real. I’m out. Toss the phial at her, and teleport the fuck out of there. Enjoy, Asuna, hope that helps…all this *gestures wildly*

God, people take these games way too seriously.

Anyway, think I’ll switch back to my cat-boi and go troll some poor sprouts in MSQ rolo. The Discord’s gonna LOVE this.

About:

This was the last story I wrote for the collection, and I envision it as kind of a counter-argument to the other stories, which generally take the player/character relationship quite seriously. Choco’s player sees no relationship between her gender and her character’s identity. As she says, it’s a big dumb game, and her use of fantasia is purely for transactional purposes. While it’s definitely the most comedic story, I think this perspective of irreverence is actually possibly the most queer of them all, since while Choco doesn’t occupy as obviously liminal a gender/sexuality as, say, Red, Choco is, at all times, all races and all genders, with no significance attached to any of them, which throws any semblance of normativity out the window.

I think this is also a good time to touch on how I chose the look of each of these characters (considering I used fantasia on my character several times in order to capture story screenshots). For most of the characters, this was pretty easy, since I used in-story descriptions I wrote to model them, or based them on the characters' or players' personalities. Choco presented a unique challenge in that, in essence, his entire existence is, for lack of a better term, designed to take the piss out of the game's systems, utilizing the same freedoms that Red criticizes in the next story to his benefit. In the customization menu I chose the most ridiculous and maximalist options available, to create a character that a player would create as a joke at the game's expense, which leads to an interesting discussion of what gender and racial expressions both the game and the player are inclined to treat as jokes or unrealistic (though discussing realism in a fantasy game is perhaps best left to "Millions"). Is the bouffant hairstyle I chose meant to be treated as a joke, or are there players who attach a certain gender significance to that hairstyle? It reads to me as a sort of foppish, over the top masculinity, but even the concept of 'foppish' is something that needs to be dissected in how it acts as a pejorative toward certain expressions of masculinity.
